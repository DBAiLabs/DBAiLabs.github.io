The Craft

Made the
slow way.
On purpose.

01
Movement first, always
Every piece begins with a Swiss ETA calibre serviced, regulated, and certified to ±2 seconds per day — before the case is ever touched. The movement is the watch. Everything else is clothing.
02
Surfaces finished by hand
The alternation between brushed and polished surfaces on a case is where a watchmaker's skill is most legible. We spend eight hours on finishing alone — no machines, no shortcuts.
03
One name on every caseback
From movement service to final timing test, your watch is touched by one pair of hands. That craftsman's initials are engraved on the caseback. Because accountability is also a form of craft.
